IT Networks

Common network topologies at a glance.

Short, practical summaries of how each topology is structured, its strengths and tradeoffs, and where it shows up today.

1. Bus Topology

Used today? Mostly obsolete

All devices share a single central cable (the bus).

Pros

  • Cheap
  • Simple to set up
  • Minimal cabling

Cons

  • One cable failure breaks everything
  • Poor performance as devices increase
  • Hard to troubleshoot

2. Star Topology

Used today? Most common

All devices connect to a central switch or hub.

Pros

  • Easy to manage
  • One device failure does not affect others
  • Scales well

Cons

  • Central device is a single point of failure
  • More cabling than bus

3. Ring Topology

Used today? Rare

Devices form a closed loop; data travels in one or both directions.

Pros

  • Predictable performance
  • No collisions

Cons

  • One break can stop the ring
  • Difficult to reconfigure

4. Mesh Topology

Used today? Yes

Devices connect to many or all other devices.

Pros

  • Very high reliability
  • Multiple paths for data
  • Excellent fault tolerance

Cons

  • Expensive
  • Complex to manage
  • Lots of cabling or radio links

5. Tree (Hierarchical) Topology

Used today? Yes

Star networks connected in layers (root to branches to leaves).

Pros

  • Highly scalable
  • Organized structure
  • Easy segmentation

Cons

  • Root failure affects many nodes
  • More complex setup

6. Hybrid Topology

Used today? Yes

Combination of two or more topologies (for example, star + mesh).

Pros

  • Flexible
  • Optimized for real needs
  • Scales well

Cons

  • Design complexity
  • Higher cost

7. Point-to-Point Topology

Used today? Yes

Direct link between two devices.

Pros

  • Simple
  • Fast
  • Secure

Cons

  • Not scalable
